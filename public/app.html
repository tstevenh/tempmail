    What is temp mail?

    Temp mail is a free, disposable email address you can use for a short time and then throw away. It works like a normal inbox — it receives real messages in real time — but it isn't tied to your identity and requires no sign-up, so you can hand it out anywhere without exposing your personal email.

    People reach for a temporary email whenever a website demands an address just to let them in: free downloads, trials, forums, coupon codes, app testing, or one-off verification. Instead of giving away your real inbox — and inviting months of spam and marketing — you give away a throwaway address, grab the confirmation link or OTP code, and move on. When you're done, you simply stop using it.

    Every address on this page runs on real mail-server infrastructure, so verification emails and one-time passwords usually arrive within seconds. There's nothing to install and no account to create: your disposable inbox is live the moment the page loads, and a fresh address is one click away.

    Why use a disposable email address?

    A disposable email address is one of the simplest privacy habits you can build. It puts a buffer between your real identity and the dozens of services that ask for an email every month.

    • Stop spam before it starts. Marketing blasts and newsletters go to the throwaway address, never your real inbox.
    • Receive OTP & verification codes instantly. Most one-time passwords and confirmation links land within seconds, so you can finish any sign-up.
    • Protect your privacy. Keep your identity, contacts, and primary inbox separate from random websites you may never visit again.
    • Survive data breaches. If a site you used a temp address on leaks its database, your real email and the accounts tied to it aren't exposed.
    • No commitment. No registration, no password to remember, and nothing to clean up later — the address disappears on its own.

    When should you use a temporary email?

    A temp mail address is the right tool any time an email is required but a relationship isn't. Common examples:

    • Free trials and downloads. Unlock an ebook, file, or trial without joining a mailing list.
    • One-time sign-ups. Forums, wifi portals, comment sections, and sites you'll use once.
    • Testing your own product. Developers use disposable inboxes to test registration, OTP, and email flows.
    • Coupons and promos. Claim a discount code without handing over your main address.
    • Avoiding "verify your email" friction. Get the code, confirm, and get on with what you came to do.

    For anything you'll need long-term — banking, work, government, or accounts you must be able to recover — use your real email instead. A temporary inbox is for the throwaway moments, not your identity.

    Other names for temporary email

    Temp mail goes by many names, and they all describe the same idea — a free, no-sign-up inbox you use briefly and discard:

    • Disposable email — emphasises that the address is meant to be thrown away.
    • Throwaway email — a one-and-done address for a single sign-up.
    • Burner email — like a burner phone, used once then abandoned.
    • Fake email / fake email generator — a working address that isn't your real one (the messages are real, though).
    • Anonymous email — an address not tied to your name or identity.
    • 10 minute mail — a temporary inbox you only need for a few minutes.
    • DEA (disposable email address) — the technical term used by developers.

    How to choose a temp mail service

    Not every disposable email tool is equal. A good temp mail service should be:

    • Real-time. Messages and OTP codes should arrive in seconds, not minutes.
    • Free and sign-up free. You shouldn't need an account, a card, or an app to receive one email.
    • Multi-domain. Several domains to choose from, so you can switch if a site blocks one.
    • Clean and readable. A simple inbox that shows the message and lets you copy codes fast.
    • Private by design. No tracking of who you are, and inboxes that clear themselves automatically.

    This service ticks every box: instant delivery, no registration, multiple domains, and a distraction-free inbox that updates the moment a message arrives.

    Is temp mail safe?

    Used the right way, temp mail is safe — and often safer than giving out your real address. It keeps your personal inbox private, cuts spam, and limits how much of your data spreads across the web. If a temporary address is ever compromised, you lose nothing: you just generate a new one.

    The important caveat: a disposable inbox is not a private vault. Messages are temporary and may be readable by anyone who knows or guesses the address, and inboxes are cleared on a schedule. Never use temp mail to receive bank statements, passwords for important accounts, medical records, or anything sensitive or long-term. Treat every message as public and temporary, and it's an excellent everyday privacy tool.
